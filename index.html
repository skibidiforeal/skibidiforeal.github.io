Autobiography

Who is Anarxy?

My name is Anarxy, and I’ve always seen the world differently. I see the connections beneath the surface, the ones that bind technology, data, and the unseen world of digital information together. From a young age, I was drawn to computers, fascinated by how they work, by how lines of code could turn into something meaningful and powerful. While most people look at technology as a tool, I see it as an art form — one I can manipulate and shape.

As a hacker, I have developed skills that are more than just breaking through security systems or writing scripts. Hacking, for me, is about understanding the fabric of the digital universe. It’s about finding loopholes, exploring the boundaries of what’s possible, and using my knowledge to push past limitations. But with all my potential, my life has been nothing short of a constant battle against the restrictions that surround me.

The Origins of My Skills

I didn’t come from a privileged background. My family wasn’t wealthy, and we certainly didn’t have the latest tech gadgets in the house. What we did have was an older, slow computer That computer became my first teacher. I tinkered with it, broke it, fixed it, learned from it, and eventually mastered it. Through trial and error, I taught myself programming languages, network protocols, and how to exploit weaknesses in software.

As I grew older, my hunger for knowledge only intensified. I tried participating in online communities, making friends with other like-minded individuals who shared my curiosity for how things worked behind the scenes, but really only a few chose me, even then, im not sure if they even like me. We explored the digital world, sometimes diving into areas where we weren’t exactly welcome. But for me, it was never about malice. It was about proving to myself what I could achieve.

The Restriction

But despite my abilities, home life has always been my biggest barrier. It's like living with chains that I can't break free from. My family doesn’t understand what I do or why I’m passionate about it. They see hacking as something bad, something to be feared. No matter how many times I’ve tried to explain that hacking isn’t inherently evil, that it’s about creativity and pushing the boundaries of what’s possible, they just don’t get it.

The lack of understanding turns into restrictions. I’m not allowed the freedom to fully immerse myself in my work. I can’t stay up late to code, I can’t spend hours on projects that have the potential to make a real impact. My home environment is suffocating, and it stifles my progress. I feel like I’m trapped, unable to move forward, unable to break free.

Help

This blog is my cry for help. I can’t do this alone. I’m stuck in a life where no one listens to me, no one understands me, and no one cares about the skills I’ve worked so hard to develop. I want to be heard, I want my story to reach someone who can help me break free of these chains. My home life is holding me back, and it’s becoming unbearable. Every day feels like another missed opportunity, another step further away from realizing my full potential.

If you’re reading this, I need you to understand that I can’t do this alone. I need someone to listen to me, to guide me, to give me a chance to show the world what I’m capable of. My talents as a hacker are being wasted because I’m stuck in a life that doesn’t allow me to grow. This is more than just a story — this is a plea for help. I don’t know how much longer I can keep fighting on my own. At this point, I am fighting myself
